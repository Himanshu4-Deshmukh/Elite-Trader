2. Two Candlestick Pattern



i. Bullish Engulfing Pattern -


It is a bullish reversal pattern with two candles that indicates the end of the current downtrend and the beginning of an uptrend.

In this pattern, the first day is generally a small or red bearish candle that forms in line with the ongoing bearish trend, followed by a long white or green bullish candle that completely engulfs the real body of the previous day's bearish candle.


This candlestick pattern demonstrates that buyers have been prevailing over sellers and are more likely to take the price upward.




ii. Bearish Engulfing Pattern -


It is a bearish reversal pattern with two candles that signals the end of an uptrend and the beginning of a downtrend.

The first day of this pattern typically consists of a long black or red bearish candle that completely engulfs the actual body of the previous day's bullish candle. The second day typically consists of a small white or green bullish candle that forms in line with the ongoing bullish trend.


This candlestick pattern demonstrates that sellers have dominated buyers and are more likely to price downward.




iii. Bullish Harami Pattern -


Bullish Harami is a bullish reversal pattern with two candles that indicates the end of a downtrend and the beginning of an uptrend.


Pattern derives its name the japanese word Harami which means a "Pregnant Woman" because the graphic that represents this pattern resembles a pregnant woman.

On the first day of this pattern, a large red or black bearish candle usually forms in line with the current bearish trend. On the second day, a smaller green or white bullish candle usually forms with a smaller body and smaller lower and upper shadows.

The second candle's body is contained within the first candle's body.


Ideally, the size of the body of the larger candle should be approximately 4 times the size of the body of the smaller candle.

If the upper and lower shadows of the white or green candle do not extend beyond the black or red candle, the pattern will look better.




iv. Bearish Harami Pattern -


A bearish reversal pattern known as a bearish harami is a two- candle pattern that indicates the end of an uptrend and the beginning of a downtrend.

The first day of this pattern typically has a large green or white bullish candle with a small body and comparatively smaller lower and upper shadows that aligns with the ongoing bullish trend.

The second candle's body is contained within the first candle's body.

Ideally, the size of the body of the larger candle should be approximately 4 times the size of the body of the smaller candle.

If the upper and lower shadows of the red or black candle do not extend beyond the green or white candle, the pattern will look better.

This pattern suggests that sellers are taking control and ready to take the price lower.




v. Piercing Line Pattern-


The Piercing Line Pattern is a bullish reversal pattern with two candles that signals the end of a downtrend and the beginning of an uptrend.

A large red or black bearish candle typically forms on the first day of this pattern, following a strong green or white bullish candle that opens lower than the previous day's low but manages to close above the midrange of the previous candle.

It is considered to be a more bullish signal if the volume of the second candle is higher than the average volume.

This candlestick pattern demonstrates that buyers have begun to dominate sellers and are more likely to accept a higher price.




vi. Dark Cloud Cover Pattern-

Dark cloud cover is a two-candle pattern that marks the beginning of a downtrend and the end of an uptrend.

In this pattern, the first day is generally a large green or white bullish candle that forms in line with the ongoing bullish trend, followed by a strong red or black bearish candle that opens higher than the previous days high but eventually manages to the close below the mid range of the previous candle.

It is thought to be a more bearish signal if the volume on the second candle is higher than the average volume.

This candlestick pattern demonstrates that sellers have begun to dominate buyers and are more likely to accept a lower price.





3. Three Candlestick Pattern


i. Morning Star Pattern-


The morning star pattern is a bullish reversal pattern with three candles that indicates the end of a downtrend and the beginning of an uptrend.

The first day of this pattern typically consists of a gap down small body candle and a large red or black bearish candle that form in line with the ongoing bearish trend. This second candle has the potential to be either bullish or bearish. The third candle is a gap up long green or white bullish candle that closes close to or above the first candle's midrange.


This candlestick pattern demonstrates how buyers gained market dominance over sellers. It is thought that this pattern is a strong indication of a trend reversal when it occurs near a support level at the bottom of a downtrend.


It is considered to be a more bullish signal if the volume on the third candle is higher than the average volume.




ii. Evening Star Pattern-


The evening star pattern is a bearish reversal pattern with three candles that indicates the end of an uptrend and the beginning of a downtrend.

The first day of this pattern typically consists of a large green or white bullish candle and a gap up small body candle that form in line with the ongoing bullish trend. This second candle has the potential to be either bullish or bearish. The third candle is a gap down long red or black bearish candle that closes close to or below the first candle's midrange.


This candlestick pattern shows how sellers look control of the market from buyers. When this pattern occurs at the top of an uptrend near a resistance level, it is interpreted as a powerful trend reversal signal.


If the volume on the third candle is higher than the average volume then it is considered to be a stronger bearish signal.




iii. Three White Soldiers Pattern-


Three white soldiers is a bullish reversal pattern with three candles that indicates the end of a downtrend and the beginning of an uptrend. Buyers are taking control, as evidenced by this pattern, and the price is anticipated to rise.

The open and close of each of the three candles in this pattern are above those of the previous candle, making them all long bullish candles.


The fact that each of the three candles is nearly the same length and all three candles have comparatively smaller shadows.




iv. Three Black Crows Pattern-


Three Black Crows is a bearish reversal pattern with three candles that signals the end of an uptrend and the beginning of a downtrend. Price is anticipated to fall as a result of this pattern, which indicates that sellers are taking control.

The open and close of each of the three candles in this pattern are below those of the previous candle, making them all long bearish candles. The fact that all three candles are nearly the same length and cast shadows that are comparatively smaller is another important aspect of this pattern.




Important Remarks


Although candlestick patterns capture the attention of market players, they should never be traded upon in isolation. To confirm the overall trend, they should be used in conjunction with other technical analysis tools.

For instance, if you are looking for additional confirmation and are focusing on the security's volume, a trading signal that uses a candlestick pattern and is accompanied by rising volume is considered to be very strong.


Although we are not concerned with technical indicators as Price Action traders, a Moving Average is still recommended for additional confirmation.

The fact that there is no best or worst candlestick pattern is another thing to keep in mind. You must establish patterns that suit you and then adhere to them.


If you are just starting out, I would suggest that you look at more charts and try to recognize candlestick patterns in order to train your eyes to easily recognize them.
