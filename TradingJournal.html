What is Trading Journal ?


For traders, a trading journal is an essential tool for monitoring and evaluating their trades. It is a written record of every trade a trader makes, including the reason for the trade, the outcome, the entry and exit points, and the size of the position. How to make a trading journal is as follows:


  • Choose a format:

    • A spreadsheet or a notebook can be used to create a trading journal. Make sure that the format you choose is simple to use and easy to access.


  • Record every trade:

    • For each trade you make, write down the date, time, symbol, position size, entry and exit prices, and any other pertinent information.


  • Analyze your performance:

    • Examine your trading journal on a regular basis to look for patterns and evaluate your performance. Make adjustments to your trading strategy based on the areas in which you can grow and develop.


  • Be honest:

    • Be open with yourself about the outcomes of your trades. Don't try to cover up losses or make up for gains.


  • Include notes:

    • Write down your thoughts and feelings in your journal before, during, and after each trade. This can assist you in identifying any emotional patterns or biases that might be influencing your trading decisions.


  • Use it as a learning tool:

    • You should use your trading journal as a learning tool to improve your trading abilities. Use it to distinguish botches, gain from them, and pursue better choices later on.



    In general, traders of all levels can benefit greatly from using a trading journal. It enables you to monitor your progress, gain knowledge from your mistakes, and improve your future trading decisions.
