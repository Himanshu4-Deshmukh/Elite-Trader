vi.Falling and Rising, Support and Resistance

Support and Resistance are the important price points on the chart of any security (stock) at which the security is expected to attract the maximum amount of Buying and Selling.

They are the points where there is a maximum probality of a pause or a reversal, of a prevailing trend . The logic behing them is demand and supply.

When the demand (Buyers) is more than the Supply (Sellers) price of the security (stock) increases.
And When the Supply (sellers) is more than the demand (Buyers) price of the security (stock) decreases.

Most of the Traders use the concept of support and resistance as an important factor in the market.


i. What is Support?


Support is a level at which the price of a security (stock) does not fall further because the supply is sufficient enough to hold the price from rising furthere.

When supply is more than demand price of the security decreases. Now as a price which is near the support level more and more buyes get keen to buy the security whereby demand becomes more than the supply and hence price stop falling further and start rising

Support will always appear or form below the current market price of a security


ii. What is a Resistance?


Resistance is a level at which the price does not Rice further because the demand is sufficient enough to hold the price from rising furthere.

When demand is more than supply price of the security increases. Now as a price which is near the Resistance level more and more sellers get keen to sell the security whereby supply becomes more than the demand and hence price stop falling further and start falling

Resistance will always appear or form above the current market price of a security


iii. How to Plot Support and Resistance level?


  • First of look at the past price data of security (stock) whose support or resistance level you want to find.
  • for Support - find the extreme lows price point from where the price of security turns in upword direction
  • for resistance - find the extreme highs price point from where the price of security turns in downward direction
  • Connect all this price point with horizontal straight line, try to find at list 3 or more touches to horizontal line

    • iv. Why we use Support and Resistance


    By identifying support and resistance levels, traders can develop trading strategies based on the expected price movements at these levels. For example, a trader may look to buy an asset near a support level with the expectation that the price will bounce back up, or they may look to sell an asset near a resistance level with the expectation that the price will reverse and head back down. But it is used alongwith candelsticks pattern, chart pattern, or Indicators, for better conformation.


    v. -Support becomes Resistance and

    - Resistance becomes Support


    When support becomes resistance and resistance becomes support this is known as The Principal Of Polarity

    The reversal of support and resistance occurs when the price of security is finally able to break out and go beyond an support and resistance level.

    A broken support level becomes a future resistance level and similarly A broken resistance level becomes a future support level.

    A trader may look to sell when support level is break with the expectation that the price will fall upto the next support level or key level, or they may look to buy an asset after the breakout of resistance level with the expectation that the price will rice up to next resistance level or key level. But it is used alongwith candelsticks pattern, chart pattern, or Indicators, for better conformation.


    vi. Rising Support

    When the price of a stock rises over time, it will typically form a pattern of higher highs and higher lows. The rising support line is drawn by connecting the series of higher lows with a trendline (Rising Support Line), and it can help traders identify potential buy points or areas of support for the stock.



    Rising Resistance

    When the price of a stock rises over time, it will typically form a pattern of higher highs and higher lows. The rising Resistance line is drawn by connecting the series of higher Highs with a trendline (Rising Resistance Line), and it can help traders identify potential Sell points or areas of support for the stock.

    Falling Support

    When the price of a stock Falls over time, it will typically form a pattern of Lower Highs and Lower Lows. The Support line is drawn by connecting the series of Lower Lows with a trendline (falling support Line), and it can help traders identify potential Buy points or areas of support for the stock.
