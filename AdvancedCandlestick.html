5. Advanced Candlestick Analysis



In this chapter, I'll talk about how experienced traders analyze these candles to extract useful information and trade accordingly.

Candlesticks not only show the open, high, low, and close prices, but they also tell a lot about what buyers and sellers are doing, their strength, the strength of a trend, momentum, etc. if they are properly analyzed in conjunction with volume.


Let me demonstrate a method for visualizing candles employing the concept of "Path of Price" before we get into the specifics of how to analyze candles. Candlesticks are made simple to understand by this.


A path of price is nothing more than the process by which a candle is formed; for instance, if you notice that the price of a security opened at Rs. 50 and decreased below Rs 40 before achieving a high of Rs. 75 and finishing the trading session with a price of Rs. 70 then, when represented by a candlestick, this kind of path of price information becomes a bullish candle formation.


Simply put, a path of price reveals the psychological factors that led to the formation of a bullish or bearish candlestick.


We need to pay close attention to four things in order to fully investigate candlesticks:


Detailed Candlestick Analysis -

  • 1. Size of the Candlestick Body
  • 2. Length of the Shadow
  • 3. Body to Shadow Ratio
  • 4. Position of the Candlestick Body

Important Remark:-
Use all of the above aspects of the candlestick together to get a complete picture of what's going on instead of just one or two of them.



1. Size of the Candlestick Body -


In a bullish or bearish environment, the size of the candlestick's body indicates how strong the dominance is.


A long body on a candlestick indicates strong buying or selling pressure and heavy trading in one direction. (a sign of strength in itself).


A short candlestick body indicates little buying or selling pressure and little trading in one direction. (a strong indication of weakness).


While taking a long or short trading position just keep in mind that if the body of the candle is wide then the probability of the trade going in your favor will become more and vice-versa.



The momentum of the current trend is increasing if the size of candlestick bodies increases over time (preferably a few consecutive candles). Never trade against the trend during this time.



If the size of candlestick bodies decreases over a period of time (preferably a few consecutive candles) then it indicates momentum of the prevailing trend is decreasing and it may soon come to an end. During such periods, watch out for reversal signs.


If candlestick bodies remain more or less constant then it indicates a steady trend.




2. Length of the Candlestick Body -


Short shadows may indicate a stable market with less uncertainty, while long shadows may indicate uncertainty.


The selling pressure or rejection of a higher price can be seen in the upper shadow.

The shadow that is lower indicates that buyers are under pressure to buy or that they are rejecting a lower price.




3. Body to Shadow Ratio -


During strong trends, candlestick bodies are significantly longer than shadows.


Candlestick shadows begin to grow larger than candlestick bodies as the trend weakens.


Candlesticks have long shadows and short bodies when there is sideways movement, consolidation, or when the trend is about to reverse.




4. Position of the Candlestick Body -


If the body of a bullish candle is on the top end, it is considered to be more bullish, indicating that buying pressure is greater than selling pressure.



If the body of a bearish candle is on the bottom end, it is considered to be more bearish, indicating that selling pressure is greater than buying pressure.
