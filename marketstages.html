2. Market Stages or Market Cycle


The time between two recent highs or lows for any security (stock, bond, commodity, currency, etc) is referred to as the market cycle. Simply put, it refers to the phases during which any security's price fluctuates.


Market participants who trade in the theme are interested in learning the patterns through which prices typically move, despite the fact that the price of the security goes up and down repeatedly. These price patterns are known as stages of market.


A trader who is able to recognize each of these phases or stages and adapts his trading style accordingly will undoubtedly be profitable.


In the market, it has been observed that traders usually act in a certain way at certain times, which can be captured during important market phases. As a result, one can determine the manner in which the majority of market participants trade securities by studying the market's structure.


Main Stages of a Market Cycle:

  • Accumulation Phase
  • Advancing Phase
  • Distribution Phase
  • Declining Phase


    • Accumulation Phase

    The first stage of every market cycle is the accumulation phase. After the declining phase of the previous cycle is over, the security enters a consolidation phase at this point. When a security is trading within a predetermined range, people begin to accumulate it during this stage.


    Accumulation phase occurs before an uptrend but after a downward trend therefore, this phase is also referred to as the basic period. This phase's promotion feature is an increase in trading volume but not in price.



    Advancing Phase

    The security enters the advancing stage after breaking out of the accumulation stage, indicating that the uptrend will continue to be strong. It also indicate that the majority of people are currently bullish on the security.



    Distribution Phase

    The security begins to lose momentum during this phase, and traders begin to book profits, indicating that buyers are withdrawing and that short positions are being held in the security. The security may trade within a limited range during this phase as well, just like it did during the accumulation phase.



    Declining Phase

    The security moves out of the distribution stage and into the declining stage, indicating that the downtrend will continue to be strong. It demonstrates that the majority of people are currently bearish on security. The market is in its final phase, making it unfavorable for the majority of investors. This phase is characterized by a significant price decline and an increase in trading volume.
