A trader always study the behavior of buyes and sellers using the historical price movements in order to determine the best course of action. Therefore it is very important to study the tranding and ranging market.


When market are trading in an uptrend or a downtrend it is said to be a Trending Market. when it is trades in a sideways trend it is said to be a Ranging Market.




Trending Market


A market that is trending typically shows price movement in upward or downward direction.


Small corrections or pullbacks or retracements are part of a trending market, but when you look at the big picture or over a long time frame, you will see that the market is moving in a clear direction.


You can use any time frame, such as daily, weekly, or monthly, depending on your trading style (such as scalping, intraday trading, swing trading, positional trading, short term trading, long term investing, etc.), to identify a trending market. There is no specific time frame for identifying a trending market. However, keep in mind that the trend will look better the longer you use the time frame.


When a price of security forms a series of higher highs and higher lows (HH&HL Structure), the market is said to be trending in an upward.



When a price of security forms a series of lower lows and lower highs (LL&LH Structure), the market is said to be trending in an downtrend.

Typically, a trending market indicates that participants in the market have become bullish (up trending) or bearish (down trending), which can be easily identified by examining the chart.


It is crucial for us to know when the market is trending and when it is ranging because the market will be in a clear trend 30% of the time and will be range bound 70% of the time.


How to Identify Trending Market?


Step 1 : Look at the Market Structure


  • Higher High and Higher Low Structure (Uptrend) -

    • The structure indicates the uptrend and that individuals are prepared to buy on every Dip.


  • Lower Low and Lower High Structure (Downtrend) -

    • The structure indicates the downtrend and that individuals are prepared to sell on every Rice.


    Step 2 : Look at Moving Average to get Additional Information


  • 200 Day moving Average (DMA) -

    • Trading in an uptrend is considered bullish if the market is trending above the 200 DMA. In a similar vein, a security is considered to be bearish, or in a downtrend, if it is trading below the 200 DMA.

    How to Trade in Trending Market -

  • When the market is in an uptrend, buy at support and sell at resistance.
  • When the market is in an downtrend, sell at resistance and buyback at support.
  • When a market is trending, it's always best to follow the trend; if you're just starting out, don't try to break with it.


    • Never Do This -

  • In a market with an upward trend, you should never sell at resistance and buy back at support.
  • In a market with a downward trend, you should never buy at support and sell at resistance.



    • Ranging Market


    Price consolidation within a range is known as a ranging market. Price frequently moves back and forth between support and resistance levels in such a market environment. Consolidation, congestion phase, range bound, choppy, sideways, and flat market are all other names for it.


    The price can't go up any more because of the resistance, and the price can't go down any more because of the support. As a result, the security's price fluctuates within a range.


    The fact that buyers and sellers are in a state of balance and equilibrium is the primary reason for the fluctuating market. The trending market is completely different from the ranging market.



    How to Identify the Ranging Market?

  • Look at the Market Structure -

    • Draw support and resistance lines or zones and find that price moves back and forth between the support and resistance level, a number of times.


  • Look at the Volume to get an Additional Confirmation -

    • The volume is the next thing to examine after the market structure. When a security's price enters the consolidation phase, as opposed to when it was trending up or down, the volume decreases.



    When the price breaks through either support or resistance in a ranging market, it signals the beginning of either an uptrend or a downtrend.

    How to Trade in Ranging Market


    There are two ways to make trades in a ranging market.


  • Swing Trading -You must trade under the consolidation, which allows you to buy the security at the support level and sell it at the resistance level, and vice versa.

  • Breakout Trading - Once a security breaks through the resistance or support level, you can buy or sell it.
